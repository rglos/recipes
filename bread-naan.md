# Naan Bread

## Ingredients


### Dry

- 500g flour
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon baking powder

### Wet

- 240g milk warmed
- 30g olive oil
- 1 packet of active dried yeast, or 7 grams
- 60g yogurt
- 2 teaspoons sugar

## Scaling recipe

| Ingredient         | Half   | Original  | 1.5x  | Double |
|-------------------|--------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Dry Ingredients**|        |         |         |           |
| Flour             | 250g   | 500g    | 750g    | 1000g     |
| Salt              | 1 tsp  | 2 tsp   | 3 tsp   | 4 tsp     |
| Baking powder     | 1/2 tsp| 1 tsp   | 1.5 tsp | 2 tsp     |
| **Wet Ingredients**|        |         |         |           |
| Milk              | 120g   | 240g    | 360g    | 480g      |
| Olive oil         | 15g    | 30g     | 45g     | 60g       |
| Yogurt            | 30g    | 60g     | 90g     | 120g      |
| Yeast             | 1/2 tsp  | 1 tsp   | 1 1/2 tsp   | 2 tsp     |
| Sugar             | 1 tsp  | 2 tsp   | 3 tsp   | 4 tsp     |

## Directions

- In small bowl, gather dry ingredients and whisk together
- In stand mixer bowl, gather wet ingredients
- Pour dry into wet
- Place bowl in stand mixer with dough hook and mix on #2 for 4 minutes
- Let rise - 4 hours, 12 hours, 24 hours - slow it down in fridge if need be
- split into 6 pieces at ~75g each.  Take each piece, roll it into a ball and flatten it between your palms. Dust the dough with flour, and roll each piece out into an oval of about 5 by 8 inches.
- Place a large sauté pan over medium-high heat, and allow it to get hot. When it is, cook one naan in it for 30 to 40 seconds on one side, or until it begins to bubble, then use a spatula to flip it over to cook the other side for about the same amount of time, checking regularly to make sure that it does not burn. Flip the naan one more time, and press on it lightly with your spatula for 10 to 15 seconds, then remove to a warm platter. Repeat with the rest of the dough.
