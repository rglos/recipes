# Bacon - Cured

## Ingredients

| Ingredient         | 2-3 lbs   | 11-12 lbs  |
|-------------------|--------|---------|
| Pork Belly | 2-3 lbs | 11-12 lbs |
| Brown Sugar       | 1/2 cup - 100g   | 1.5 cups - 300g |
| Salt              | 3 tablespoons - 60g | 180g |
| Black Pepper     | 1 1/4 teaspoons | 3.5 tablespoons |
| Curing salt (optional) | 1/2 teaspoon | 1.5 teaspoons |

2-Gallon Ziploc Seal Top Freezer Bag - [Ziploc Seal Top Freezer Bag, 2-Gallon, 10-count, 3-pack | Costco](https://www.costco.com/p/-/ziploc-seal-top-freezer-bag-2-gallon-10-count-3-pack/4000061453)

## Directions

### The Cure

- Gather the ingredients.
- Score the fat cap of the pork belly in a cross-hatch pattern
- In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, salt, pepper, and optional curing salt. Mix well.
- Place the pork belly into a plastic bag - if using larger size from Costco, I usually have to cut the pork belly into 2 pieces
- Once in the bag, season the top and flip and season the bottom, and spread mixture around
- Seal bag and place into fridge for 10 to 14 days, turning the bag over occasionally

### The Smoke

- After the cure is completed, rinse the bacon well and again pat it thoroughly dry with paper towels
- Place onto a write rack on sheet tray in fridge for 2 hours to form a pellicle—a sticky surface layer of proteins that forms on the surface of the meat. This helps the smoke cling to it, resulting in more flavorful bacon
- Smoke the cured, air-dried bacon at approximately 200 F until it reaches an internal temperature of 150 F / 66 C, the minimum safe temperature for consumption of this product
