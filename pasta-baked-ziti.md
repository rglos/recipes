# Baked Ziti

## Ingredients

For the pasta and casserole

- 1 pound (454g) ziti, penne, or other thick tubular pasta
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 12 ounces (340g) whole-milk ricotta (see notes)
- 3 ounces (85g) Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated (about 1 1/2 cups), divided
- 2 large eggs
- 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh parsley, divided
- 3 tablespoons minced fresh basil, divided
- 1 pound (454g) low-moisture whole-milk mozzarella, cut into rough 1/4-inch cubes, divided
- Good extra-virgin olive oil, for finishing (optional but recommended)
- 1 pound ground beef or italian sausage

Sauce (choose one):

- Option A (fast + excellent): 4 cups (950ml) high-quality marinara (Rao’s is solid), divided
- Option B (make it like the video): Homemade marinara (see below), about 4–5 cups total

### Quick Marinara Sauce

- 4 tablespoons (56g) unsalted butter
- 4 tablespoons (60ml) extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped (or half of a very large onion)
- 4 medium cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 large sprig basil (stems in, leaves reserved), plus extra leaves to finish
- 2 (28-ounce / 794g) cans crushed tomatoes or whole peeled tomatoes (crush by hand)
- Kosher salt

## Directions

1. Heat oven + soak pasta

    Adjust a rack to the middle position and preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
    Place pasta in a large bowl and cover with warm salted water by 3–4 inches. Let soak 20 to 30 minutes, stirring once or twice early on to prevent sticking. Drain well.

1. Reserve sauce for the top

    If using homemade sauce, ladle out and reserve about half for topping. (If using jarred sauce, measure out about 2 cups to reserve and keep the rest for the pink base.)
    You want enough on top to mostly cover the pasta, but you don’t need a thick blanket.

1. Make the pink base

    In a large pot (or the same one you made the sauce in), combine the remaining sauce (the part not reserved) with ricotta, half the Parmigiano, eggs, heavy cream, and half the parsley and basil. Stir until smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
    Tip: If your sauce is ripping hot, let it cool briefly, or whisk eggs into ricotta/cream first so they don’t set on contact.

1. Fold in pasta + cheese pockets

    Add drained pasta to the pot and fold to coat. Add half the mozzarella cubes and fold again until everything is evenly distributed. The mixture may look a little loose/soupy at this stage.

1. Assemble

    Transfer mixture to a 9×13-inch baking dish (deep is nice). Stir gently to distribute. Spoon reserved marinara over the top, letting some pink peek through in places. Scatter with remaining mozzarella cubes.

1. Bake covered, then brown

    Cover dish tightly with foil (spray the underside with cooking spray if you want zero cheese casualties). Bake for 45 minutes. Remove foil and bake until browned and bubbling, about 15 minutes more.
    Optional: Broil for 1–2 minutes at the end for extra color (just don’t walk away.)

1. Finish + rest

    Remove from oven. Immediately sprinkle with remaining Parmigiano. Drizzle with a little really good olive oil (optional, but it adds a huge aromatic hit). Sprinkle with remaining herbs. Allow to cool 10 to 15 minutes before serving

## Notes & Variations

### Ricotta
Look for ricotta that contains only milk (or whey), salt, and a starter culture or acid. Avoid gums and stabilizers. The good stuff is thicker, tastes better, and doesn’t make your sauce watery.

### Mozzarella
Low-moisture whole-milk is the right choice for maximum stretch. Fresh mozzarella works, but it releases more water and gives a different (less “baked ziti”) texture.

### Make-ahead
Assemble up through step 5, cover, and refrigerate overnight. Add a few extra minutes to the covered bake time. Finish uncovered as usual.

### Meat
Kenji grew up with baked ziti that was mostly meatless. If you want meat, brown some Italian sausage (or ground beef) and stir it into the pink base before adding pasta.
